Canada Temporary Resident Visa

A Canada Visitor Visa, also known as a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV), is a document that allows foreign nationals to enter Canada for a temporary period of time.
It is typically issued for tourism, visiting family and friends, or other non-business purposes. TRVs are typically valid for up to six months, but can be extended under certain circumstances

To apply for a TRV, you will need to complete an application form and provide supporting documents, such as a valid passport, proof of financial support, and a letter of invitation if you are visiting family or friends. You may also be required to undergo a medical examination or provide a police certificate.

The application process for a TRV can vary depending on your country of origin and other factors, so it’s a good idea to check the Government of Canada’s website for the most up-to-date information on how to apply.

Once you have received your TRV, it will indicate the date by which you must leave Canada. If you wish to stay in Canada beyond this date, you will need to apply for an extension or a new TRV. It’s important to comply with Canadian immigration laws to avoid any negative consequences in the future.

5 Reasons you must Obtain Temporary Resident Visa Canada

Here are some reasons why you might need to obtain a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) to enter Canada:

Tourism:

If you are a foreign national who wants to visit Canada for sightseeing, visiting friends or family, or participating in recreational activities, you may need a TRV.

Business:

If you are a foreign national who wants to come to Canada for business purposes, such as attending conferences or negotiating contracts, you may need a TRV.

Study:

If you are a foreign national who wants to study at a Canadian educational institution for a period of six months or less, you may need a TRV.

Work:

If you are a foreign national who wants to work in Canada temporarily, you may need a work permit and a TRV.

Transit:

If you are a foreign national who needs to transit through Canada on your way to another country, you may need a TRV.

Canada Visitor Visa Requirements

To apply for a Canada Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa), you must meet the following requirements:
  1. Have a valid passport: Your passport must be valid for the duration of your stay in Canada.
  2. Meet the eligibility criteria: You must satisfy the immigration officer that you will leave Canada at the end of your stay, have enough money to support yourself during your stay, and have no criminal record or immigration violations.
  3. Submit supporting documents: You must provide a completed application form, a passport-sized photo, and any additional documents required to support your application, such as proof of employment or funds, travel itinerary, and a letter of invitation from a Canadian host.
  4. Pay the application fee: You must pay the application fee when you submit your application.
  5. Attend an interview: You may be required to attend an interview with a Canadian visa officer to discuss your application.

What is a Canada Visitor Visa Document

A Canada Visitor Visa document also called a Temporary Resident Visa is an official document that is attached to your passport (International Passport) which shows that you meet the requirements needed to enter Canada.

Canada Visitor Visa Validity

A Canada Visitor’s Visa is allowed a period of six (6) months in Canada before it expires and renewal. In entering the port, you may be allowed to stay for a period of six (6) months by the services officer. You can check the validity date of your visit in your passport. Other documents called a visitor record, which will show the date you need to leave by is also given to you.

You are allowed a period of six (6) months if you don’t get a stamp in your passport, you can stay for 6 months from the day you entered Canada or until your passport expires, whichever comes first. If you need a stamp, you can ask a border services officer for one. If you arrive at an airport that uses primary inspection kiosks, ask the border officer after you finish at the kiosk.

Canada Visitor Visa Fees

The fees for Canada visa applications can vary depending on the type of visa you are applying for and your country of origin. Here are some general fee ranges (in Canadian dollars):
  • Temporary Resident Visa (visitor visa): $100 – $500
  • Study permit: $150 – $500
  • Work permit: $155 – $1,155
  • Permanent residency: $1,325 – $2,225

Temporary Visitor Visa Eligibility

Applying as a visitor to Canada, you must meet some basic requirements to get a visitor visa.

  • Applicant must have a valid travel document, like a passport
  • Applicant must be in good health
  • Applicant must have no criminal or immigration-related convictions
  • Applicant must convince an immigration officer that you have ties—such as a job, home, financial assets or family—that will take you back to your home country
  • Applicant must convince an immigration officer that you will leave Canada at the end of your visit
  • Applicant must have enough money for your stay
  • Applicant must have the amount of money that will sustain them depending on how long you will stay and if you will stay in a hotel, or with friends or relatives.
  • The applicant may also need a Medical Exam and a Letter of Invitation from someone who lives in Canada.

What to Do if your Temporary Resident Visa Canada Application Is Rejected?

Yes, it is possible for your Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) Canada application to be rejected. There are several reasons why an application might be rejected, including:

Incomplete or incorrect information

If you do not provide all the required information or if the information you provide is inaccurate, your application may be rejected.

Ineligibility:

If you do not meet the eligibility requirements for a TRV, your application may be rejected. For example, if you have a criminal record, a medical condition that poses a danger to public health, or insufficient funds to support your stay in Canada, you may be deemed ineligible.

Insufficient supporting documents:

If you do not provide all the required supporting documents, such as a valid passport, proof of financial support, or a letter of invitation (if applicable), your application may be rejected.

Failure to attend biometric appointments:

If you are required to provide biometric information (fingerprints and a photo), but you fail to attend the appointment, your application may be rejected.

Previous immigration violations:

If you have violated Canadian immigration laws in the past, such as overstaying a previous visa, your application may be rejected.

If your TRV application is rejected, you may have the option to appeal the decision or reapply. It’s important to carefully review the reasons for the rejection and seek the advice of a qualified immigration lawyer before deciding on your next steps.

Can I Convert Temporary Resident Visa to Permanent Visa

It is possible to apply to convert your Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) to a permanent visa in certain circumstances.

If you are in Canada on a TRV, you may be eligible to apply for permanent residency through one of the immigration programs offered by the Government of Canada, such as the Express Entry system or the Provincial Nominee Program. If you meet the eligibility requirements for one of these programs, you may be able to apply for permanent residency while you are in Canada on a TRV.

Guide to Convert Canada Visitor Visa to Work Permit

If you are currently in Canada on a Canada Visitor Visa (Temporary Resident Visa) and you want to work in Canada, you may be able to apply for a work permit under certain circumstances. Here is a general guide on how to apply for a work permit from a visitor visa:

Determine if you are Eligible:

To be eligible for a work permit, you must have a valid job offer from a Canadian employer, meet certain eligibility requirements, and obtain a positive Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) or an LMIA exemption.

Apply for a work permit:

Once you have a job offer and the necessary documentation, you can apply for a work permit. Depending on your situation, you may be able to apply online or by mail.

Wait for a decision:

It can take several weeks or months for a work permit application to be processed. During this time, you may be able to stay in Canada as a visitor, but you cannot work until your work permit is approved.

Start working:

Once your work permit is approved, you can start working for your Canadian employer.
