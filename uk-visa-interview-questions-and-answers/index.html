Another important interview that is conducted by the UKVI (UK Visa and Immigration) is the credibility interview, which is also known as the visa interview. The majority of Bangladeshi students now receive UK student visas without having to go through visa interviews. However, some students must attend the visa interview in person, via Skype or Zoom.

We have covered the possible questions and answers for a UK visa interview (both a PRE-CAS interview and a credibility interview, also known as a visa interview) in this guide. You will acquire sufficient interview-preparation ideas here.

Questions for Interviews by University

The following is the essential information for an interview for admission to a UK university.

PRE-CAS Interview: What Is It?

Students must apply for admission through CAS (Confirmation for Acceptance of Studies) in order to study in the UK. As a result, in order to be eligible for CAS, they must participate in a PRE-CAS interview. This interview is conducted online by the university’s admissions department. It will typically take about thirty minutes.

PRE-CAS Interview Sample Questions and Responses

The following are examples of PRE-CAS interview questions and responses. The university conducts the PRE-CAS Interview. For inspiration, let’s look at some examples of questions and answers.

Why do you want to go to college in the UK?

The United Kingdom is one of the most popular study destinations for international students due to its world-class universities and high-quality education. The United Kingdom is a welcoming destination for international students because of its vibrant and multicultural society. In addition, UK degrees are well-known worldwide. This is only a sample response. You can make one that stands out on its own.

Why did you select this college and program?

Clearly and concisely explain to the interviewer why you chose that university and course. Before you sit down for the interview, do some research on the university and the course.

Do you intend to employ UK workers?

I am aware of the regulations that stipulate an international student can only work 20 hours per week. In fact, my primary focus will be on my studies. I can work in accordance with the rules if I am granted additional time in addition to my studies.

What kind of job do you currently hold?

Be honest about your occupation. Simply inform them that you are only a student if you do nothing.

How do your plans for your career relate to your studies?

Specify how your studies will assist you in achieving your career objective if you have any specific career plans.

TIPS – PRE-CAS INTERVIEW

  • Throughout the interview, maintain your self-assurance.
  • Give responses that are honest, precise, and precise.
  • With proper English proficiency, answer.
  • If you don’t understand a question, politely ask the interviewer to repeat it. You could, for instance, say, “Sorry, I couldn’t hear you.” Could you please repeat the question?”
  • Provide accurate figures and facts to back up your responses.
  • Make it clear to the interviewer that you have conducted sufficient research on the university, the course, and the entire study process in the UK.

Interview for UK Visa or Credibility Interview with UKVI, Information You Need to Know

What is a Credibility Interview?

The UK Visas & Immigration (UKVI) conducts a visa interview (also known as a credibility interview) to guarantee that only genuine students will be granted a UK Student Visa. In the UK, one of the most crucial steps in the study process is a credibility interview. can be described as one of the most important steps in applying for a visa.

The responsible person will inquire about all essential information regarding studying in the UK during the Visa interview; such as your goal, interest, financial resources, career strategy, and so on.

What is the scope of a credibility check?

Your UK visa application form and interview help determine whether you are a genuine student. The following is the checklist that the interviewer will use to ask you questions.

  • Education background
  • Family background
  • Financial strength
  • Career plan (both short-term and long-term)
  • The immigration history of the student in the UK and other countries
  • Reasons for choosing a UK university as well as the course.

Who and how does the visa interview take place?

The interview for a UK visa can be conducted in person or electronically. Sometimes, you won’t even need an interview to get a visa. Additionally, a member of the UKVI (United Kingdom Visas and Immigration) staff will conduct the visa interview.
