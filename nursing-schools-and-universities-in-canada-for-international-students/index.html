Nursing Education in Canada

Official education is compulsory to become a nurse in Canada and every part of the world. You need to get hold of a two-three years diploma in nursing to start your nursing profession. However, a four-year baccalaureate degree is the ideal maximum necessity for nurses. While holding a diploma or degree in nursing or degree in nursing does not qualify you to practice nursing profession in Canada. It is only the condition for you to meet the criteria for writing the licensing examination.

University of Toronto| Nursing Schools and Universities in Canada

Address: 155 College St Suite 130, Toronto, ON M5T 1P8, Canada.

Tuition: $9,100.00 (Domestic Students), $51,660.00 (International Students).

The university is a very old known university in Ontario Canada. It started operating in 1827 and offers degree programs in a wide range of discipline to over 60,000 students. his prominent university has twisted three Governor-General of Canada, four Prime Ministers of Canada, and fourteen Justices of the Supreme court. This Canadian public university does not produce people in government, however. It also produces the best nurses in Canada. Its Lawrence S. Bloomberg Faculty of Nursing is one of the best schools of nursing in Ontario and Canada in general. QS World University ranking ranked this Canada school of nursing 2nd in the world for nursing schools. The Toronto’s University nursing programs includes a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BScN),   Master Programs, Post-Master Nurse Practitioner Diploma (PMNP), Master & Doctoral (Collaborative), and Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.).

University of Alberta| Nursing Schools and Universities in Canada

Address: Edmonton, Alberta T6G 1C9. Canada.

Tuition: $6,207.60 (Domestic Students), $25,978.40 (International Students)

Similar to UToronto, The University of Alberta or UAlberta is a different well-liked school in Canada. It began its operating as a public university in Edmonton, Alberta in 1908. The university has therefore lived for 111 years. UAlberta has also created notable alumni like Governor General Roland Michener and Prime Minister Joe Clark. Offering over 400 programs through 18 faculties, UAlberta is a global research university.

The University of Alberta’s Faculty of Nursing is the second best school of Nursing on our list. It ranked 17th in QS World Ranking for Global Nursing School. UAlberta’s Faculty of Nursing offers a diversity of undergraduate and graduate nursing programs. Its undergraduate programs comprise BScN Collaborative, BScN Bilingual, BScN Honours, After Degree Program, and Registered Psychiatric Nurse to BScN. On the other hand, UAlberta’s nursing graduate programs are the MN (Master of Nursing), Ph.D. (Doctoral Program), and the Online Ph.D. Cohort

The UBC nursing school has lived for over 100 years and it has been offering the best Ugraduate and graduate nursing program constantly to its student. McGill University| Nursing Schools and Universities in Canada

Address: 680 Sherbrooke West, Suite 1800, Montreal, QC, Canada.

Tuition: $7,940.10 (Domestic Students), $ $20,229 (International Students).

The McGill University is another top university in Canada. It started operating as a public university in Montreal in 182 and currently it offers degrees and diploma programs in over 300 academic disciplines. It has five astronauts, the present minister of Canada, and the present Governor-General of Canada. Its Ingram school of training started in 1920 and has been providing a world-class nursing education ever since. To support its mission of training future nurses and optimize the quality of health, the Ingram School of Nursing gives a Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BScN) and a Bachelor of Nursing (Integrated) BNI for undergraduate students. Master’s students, on the other hand, can apply for the MSc (A) Direct Entry, MSC (A) Nurse Bachelor Entry, MSc (A) Nurse Practitioner, and Global Health Studies. Also, Ingram School of Nursing offers a Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Nursing as well as postdoctoral studies.
